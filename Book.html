More About the Writers

Nothing is impossible. I say that with the full weight of experience behind it. I’m a storm in motion—a woman who doesn’t wait for doors to open. I find ways to walk through walls if I must. Every mission I take on becomes personal, and this one—writing my first book was one of the most intense, emotional, sleepless missions of my life.


Turbulence of Desire was born during long nights and early mornings, many of them stretched all the way until 6:00 a.m. It meant nights without sleep, endless pages of notes, scribbles on napkins, writing pads stacked high, and my laptop always by my side. My closest companion during those hours? A notebook in one hand, and Starbucks in the other. Sleep was a luxury, but telling this story felt urgent. Necessary. Writing isn’t just an art form—it’s a commitment, a sacrifice, and a calling. I couldn’t stop until the story was out of me and alive on the page.


Before I became a published author, I spent years screenwriting—learning how to build scenes that breathe and characters that stay with you. But it was my life as a training flight attendant that gave this book its pulse. Traveling the world, living out of hotel rooms, and bonding with crews from all walks of life, I gathered stories and emotions from every corner of the globe. Some nights I stayed in with my thoughts, others I chased the chaos. And some mornings I woke up wondering what the hell happened the night before. That beautiful unpredictability became the soul of Turbulence of Desire.
